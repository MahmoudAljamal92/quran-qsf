# V3.9.2 SCOPE AUDIT — full stress-test battery against the V3.8/V3.9 weak points

**Date**: 2026-04-29 morning, v7.9-cand patch H V3.9.2.
**Trigger**: post-V3.9.1 reviewer-style adversarial audit. The user asked: "what could tear our case apart?", listed four specific weak points, and asked us to run all four tests.
**Scope**: this memo records the four stress tests run on 2026-04-29 morning, their results, and what they imply for the project's headline claims.

---

## The four weak points identified by adversarial audit

1. **Hadith Bukhari was NOT in the V3.9.1 stress-test peer pool**. Hadith is the most-similar-genre Arabic peer (same Arabic, same era, similar narrative-religious style). Excluding it from the comparison was a curation choice, and including it could collapse the AUC.

2. **The CN feature is genre-defining for the Quran** — it counts the start-rate of 14 specific Arabic narrative-connectives (فاء، ثم، إذا، لما، إن، …) at verse-start. A reviewer could argue that defining "Quran's stylistic markers" as a feature, then showing the Quran has them, is partly tautological.

3. **Both EL and CN are Quran-favored** — V3.9 narrowed the rhyme claim, and CN is genre-defining. What happens if we drop BOTH features and try to distinguish the Quran from Arabic peers using only the three "genre-neutral" features (VL_CV, H_cond, T)?

4. **Feature-selection bias**: the 5 features were chosen during Phase 2 *knowing* the Quran's properties. Permutation nulls test "is this distribution random?" but they don't test "did you pick features that maximise Quran-extremism?" The deepest test is: derive classifier weights from a comparison that doesn't involve the Quran, freeze them, and apply to Quran-vs-peers.

### T1 — Hadith Bukhari inclusion

Adding 95 hadith Bukhari units to the peer pool changed nothing: AUC went from 0.9953 to 0.9954. Hadith does NOT rival the Quran in the 5-D fingerprint. Per-feature Quran-vs-hadith-only Cohen's d:

| Feature | Quran mean | Hadith mean | Cohen's d | Notes |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| EL (rhyme rate) | 0.7074 | 0.1570 | **+3.43** | Quran rhymes 4.5× more |
| VL_CV (verse-length variation) | 0.4824 | 0.7021 | **−0.83** | Hadith MORE variable than Quran (the only feature where hadith wins) |
| CN (connective start-rate) | 0.0860 | 0.0000 | **+2.09** | Hadith uses ZERO of Quran's 14 narrative-connectives at verse-start |
| H_cond (root-bigram entropy) | 0.8671 | 0.1266 | **+2.61** | Quran's lexical diversity 6.85× higher |
| T (structural tension) | −0.4824 | −3.1128 | **+3.14** | Quran's rhyme-entropy match is far higher than hadith's |

**The user's prediction was correct**: "hadith has no rhyme — it's the least; varying in length so even apples-to-apples it won't rival Quran." Confirmed empirically. Quran-vs-hadith ONLY AUC = 0.9992.

### T2 — Drop CN feature

Removing CN entirely (4-D: EL, VL_CV, H_cond, T) gave AUC = 0.9929. The CN feature contributed essentially nothing to the multivariate signal — its discriminative power was redundant with EL/VL_CV/H_cond/T. **The "Quran wins because we hand-picked Quran-genre features" argument fails empirically.**

### T3 — Drop EL+CN combined (the harshest fair test)

Dropping BOTH EL (V3.9-affected rhyme feature) AND CN (Quran-genre-defining connective feature), classifying on only (VL_CV, H_cond, T):

- T² = 770
- AUC = **0.9639**
- Cohen's d = 3.39 (still "huge" by Cohen's convention)

**This is the strongest test the project's multivariate claim has survived to date**. Even removing both potentially Quran-favored features, the Quran sits in a corner of (verse-length-variation, root-entropy, structural-tension) space that no Arabic peer occupies, at AUC 0.96.

### T4 — Quran-BLIND classifier weights

Train logistic regression weights on Iliad (Greek epic) vs Arabic Bible (religious-narrative prose), with NO Quran in training. Freeze weights. Apply to Quran-vs-Arabic-peers (in language-agnostic 5-D feature space).

- Trained weights (lang-agnostic features): `[-0.194, -0.788, 0.000, +1.759, +1.448]`
- Quran-blind AUC: **0.8202**
- Quran median score percentile (in score distribution of all units): **93.1%**

Interpretation:
- 0.82 is substantially above chance (0.5)
- The Quran sits in the 93rd percentile of score under foreign-trained weights
- **Some of the Quran-distinctiveness is intrinsic** (survives Quran-blind weighting)
- **Some of the calibration boost from 0.82 → 0.99 is genuine feature-weight tuning** that uses Quran-vs-peers training data

This is the most honest possible result: it neither validates "the Quran is uniquely extremal under any classifier" (which would be unfalsifiable), nor falsifies the multivariate claim. It says: **the Quran is genuinely extremal in a measurable, weight-tuning-partially-independent way**, with calibrated AUC ≈ 0.99 and Quran-blind AUC ≈ 0.82.

## Honest residual concerns after V3.9.2

1. **T4 AUC = 0.82 < calibrated 0.99**: the calibrated weights do contribute. A reviewer could say "your headline numbers depend on Quran-aware feature weighting." Mitigation: T4 result documented honestly; the 93rd-percentile-blind result is the more conservative claim.

2. **Real-bayt qasida data still unmeasured**: V3.9 admission stands. Future Wikipedia-scrape experiment would settle this.

3. **Cross-tradition coverage still narrow**: 7 corpora for F63/F64. Daodejing (exp112) queued. Mormon, Sikh, Bahá'í, Confucian untested.

4. **Feature-space pre-registration**: the 5 features were not pre-registered before Quran observation. This is an unfixable historical choice; future features (the universal 5-D space in F63 — VL_CV, p_max, H_EL, bigram_distinct_ratio, gzip_efficiency — was preregistered).

5. **Hadith Bukhari has only 95 units** (n_verses ≥ 15 filter would exclude many short hadith). The full Bukhari corpus has thousands of hadith; here we're using surah-equivalent chunks. **Not a confound for the AUC, but the comparison is between book-equivalents, not narration-by-narration.**
