Quran is uniquely positioned in the 2-D (H_EL, Φ_master) space

What you should see: every other corpus clusters tightly in the bottom-right (high verse-final entropy, near-zero Φ_master). The Quran sits alone in the top-left (lowest verse-final entropy, ~3 orders of magnitude higher Φ_master). The plot is on a log scale in Y; the visual gap matches the actual 965× ratio.
What the picture confirms
Across 2,509 control units in 6 Arabic families, no corpus comes within two orders of magnitude of the Quran on Φ_master. The lowest verse-final-letter entropy is 2.52 bits (Quran) vs 3.80 for the runner-up (hadith_bukhari) — a 33% gap in rhyme constraint that no other Arabic prose achieves.
Honest counterpoint
Lexical diversity (H_word) is mid-range, not extremum, on the Quran. The "highest H_word AND lowest H_EL" paradox cited in popular framings is not what the data shows. The defensible claim is uniquely low H_EL with very high Φ_master, not joint lexical-rhyme dominance.
What's still untested
Cross-language (Hebrew, Greek, Sanskrit, Avestan, Pali) Φ_master is not measured. The F58 claim is Quran-vs-Arabic-prose, not Quran-vs-all-text. Phase 3 (exp104_F53_tanakh_pilot) is pre-registered; the first verdict is BLOCKED_psalm_19_too_short (PREREG floor).
